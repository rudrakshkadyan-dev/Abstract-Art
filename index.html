ABSTRACT ART (1900–1950)

Modern art between 1900–1950 brought revolutionary experiments:

• Fauvism: Matisse, Derain — bold & vibrant colours
• Cubism: Picasso, Braque — geometric breakdown
• Futurism: Balla, Boccioni — speed & movement
• Dada: Duchamp, Tzara — anti-art rebellion
• Surrealism: Dalí, Magritte — dreams & subconscious
• Expressionism: Munch, Kirchner — emotional distortion
• Abstract Art: Kandinsky, Pollock, Mondrian — pure form & colour

MODERN POETRY (1900–1950)

• Imagism: Pound, H.D., Amy Lowell — clear images
• Vorticism: Pound, Lewis — motion & energy
• Dada Poetry: Tzara — anti-structure
• Surrealism: André Breton — subconscious writing

Important Works & Poets:

  • T.S. Eliot – “The Waste Land”
  • Ezra Pound – “The Cantos”
  • W.B. Yeats – “The Second Coming”
  • Langston Hughes – Harlem Renaissance
  • Wallace Stevens – “Collected Poems”
  • Marianne Moore – “Poems”
